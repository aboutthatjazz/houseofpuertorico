Town of the Month: ...... Juncos

Fundado en el 1797 y nombrado por la abundancia de juncos de la ribera del Río Valenciano. Entre sus barrios se encuentran: Caimito, Gurabo Arriba, Gurabo Abajo, Ceiba Norte, Ceiba Sur, Mamey, Lirios, Valenciano Arriba y Valenciano Abajo. Entre sus personalidades se encuentran: Guillermo Cotto-Thorner, Pedro José Rivera, y Clemente Pereda. Se le conoce como Los Mulos del Valenciano (equipo de pelota). Entre sus festivales se encuentra El Maratón Internacional Junqueño en noviembre.

Recipe of the Month: ......... Arroz con Gandules

A- 1 libra de gandules frescos
8 tazas de agua
4 cucharaditas de sal

B- 2 ½ onza de tocino
1 onza de jamón de cocinar
1 cebolla, mediana, mondada
1 ajó dulce, sin semillas
1 pimiento verde, sin semillas
1 tomate, mediano
3 hojas de culantro
3 ramitas de culantrillo

C- 3 cucharaditas de aceite vegetal ó manteca con achiote
3 aceitunas rellenas
½ cucharadita de alcaparras
¼ de taza de salsa de tomate

D- 2¼ tazas de arroz
3 tazas de agua

1.Enjuague bien los gandules y escúrralos. Combínelos con las 8 tazas de agua y póngalos a fuego alto. Cuando hierva, tape y cueza por 30 minutos. Agregue la sal y cueza por 15 minutos más. Escurra y reserve 3 tazas del líquido.

2. Mientras tanto, en un caldero, dore el tocino y el jamón. Añada el resto de los ingredientes incluídos en B y sofría a fuego bajo. Añada los ingredientes incluídos en C y mezcle. Lave bien el arroz, escurra y añada, mezclándolo bien. Agregue los gandules al arroz y sofría por unos minutos.

3. Añada las 3 tazas de líquido que reservó y cueza a fuego moderado hasta que seque. Voltée el arroz. Tape el caldero y cueza 30 minutos a fuego bajo. A mitad del tiempo, voltée el arroz.

!Buen Provecho!

Article of the Month: .........

Puerto Rico's Military and Memorial Day Legacies

In observance of the forthcoming Memorial Day Holiday, it is now time to reflect on the historical role played by Puerto Ricans in the various military campaigns of the United States Armed Forces.. Therefore, in the pamphlet publication entitled The Puerto Rico Self-determination Process: Toward Resolving the Political Status Dilemma, by the Citizens Educational Foundation, (May 1999), indicated that Puerto Ricans have served in the US Armed Forces since 1917. Over 200,000 have fought in all US Wars since World War I. More than 2,000 have made the supreme sacrifice. Four (4) were awarded the Congressional Medal of Honor. Roosevelt Roads Naval Base in Puerto Rico is a US Defense Cornerstone.

According to The Latino Encyclopedia (1996), the Korean War (begun in 1950) provided the U.S. Army a combat regiment consisting entirely of Puerto Ricans. Thus, soldiers of the Fighting Sixty-fifth United States Infantry, or more commonly referred to as the 65th Infantry, received a total of 125 Silver Star awards and four Distinguished Service Crosses fighting for the high ground of Chorwan (Korea). For an in-depth look of Puerto Rico's 65th Infantry, one source is W.W. Harris's Puerto Rico's Fighting 65th U.S. Infantry: from San Juan to Chorwan , (Presidio Press, San Rafael , CA, 1980, c1985). The Latino Encyclopedia indicated that more than 65,000 Puerto Ricans served in the armed forces during World War II. The 295th and 296th infantry regiments of the Puerto Rican National Guard participated in the Asian and Pacific campaigns against the Japanese. Many of the National Guards went to Europe as replacements for the 65th Regiment, which was a regular U.S. Army unit composed entirely of Puerto Ricans. Twenty members provided the ultimate sacrifice after being killed in the European campaign. Additionally, Hispanic Magazine (August 1993), indicated that the all-Hispanic 65th Infantry Division of Puerto Rico distinguished itself in Korea, participating in a number of major campaigns and earning awards, including the Presidential Unit Citation. Finally, in Puerto Rico Past and Present: An Encyclopedia, by Ronald Fernandez, et. al., (1998), stated the overwhelming fact that, in the Korean War,: "...Puerto Rico had one casualty for every 600 inhabitants as compared to one casualty for every 1,125 inhabitants in the continental United States. one out of every forty-two casualties in the Korean War was a Puerto Rican."
