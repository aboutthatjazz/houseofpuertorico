Town of the Month: ...... Juana Diaz

Fundado en el 1798, se nombró en honor a Juana Díaz, la dueña del primer hato de terreno llamado Jacagux. Sus barrios son: Amuelas, Callabo, Capitanejo, Collores, Guayabal, Jacaguas, Lomas, Río Cañas Abajo, Río Cañas Arriba, Tijera, Emajagual, Cintrona, y Sabana Llana. Entre sus personalidades se encuentran: Luis Llorens Torres, Tomás Carrión Maduro, Luis Felipe de Jesús, Cesáreo Rosa-Nieves, Mario Braschi, Arturo Gómez Costa, Lic. Herminia Tormes García, Emilia Villaronga de Armstrong, Soledad Llorens Torres, José A. Romeu, José Antonio Morales, Pedro Antonio Ocasio Bermúdez, Eladio Vega, Herminio Mercado, Ramón Tristani, Jr., Víctor Cott, José Chen Izquierdo, y el Teniente Coronel Irving Monclova Soldevila. En este pueblo se celebra la Fiesta de Reyes en enero y el Festival Carnaval El Mabí en abril.

Recipe of the Month: ......... Piña Colada

(La porción de esta receta es: 1 parte de Crema de Coco, 2 partes de ron blanco y 3 partes de jugo de piña).

      1 lata de 15 onzas de Crema de Coco
      Ron blanco de Puerto Rico
      Jugo de piña, enlatado, sin endulzar

1. En una cacerola grande, vierta el contenido de la lata de Crema de Coco.

2. Usando la misma lata como medida, llénela dos veces con ron blanco y viértalo en la cacerola.

3. Llene la lata 3 veces con jugo de piña y vierta en la cacerola.

4. En el momento de usarlo, agite bien el emvase y sirva la bebida en vasos de alrededor de 8 onzas con unos cubietos de hielo (the Old Fashioned way!). También puede servirlo al estilo frappé, virtiendo la bebida en licuadora eléctrica y batiendo con hielo picado.

Sírvalo en vaso alto, decorado con una lonja fina de pina fresca.

!Buen Provecho!

Article of the Month: ......... Puerto Rico: An Encyclopedia

In 1998 the book entitled Puerto Rico Past and Present: An Encyclopedia (by Ronald Fernandez, Serafin Mendez Mendez, and Gail Cueto), was published. As the name suggests, this is an english written, and comprehensive reference book encyclopedia that covered the last 500 years of Puerto Rican social, economic, political and cultural history. From A to Z, the book encompasses over 300 separate substantive, and fact-specific, entries covering the myriad of life in Puerto Rico covering five centuries. In this millennium year 2000, it is therefore an appropriate time to reflect upon Puerto Rico and its peoples' historical record, and its achievements.

While of course focusing on the creative and successful achievements of the Puerto Rican people, it has the invaluable and educational function of having a most comprehensive bibliographic sources for each of the over 300 entries. Therefore, as an encyclopedia, written in english, this reference source is most practical and useful for both Boricua parents, as well as for their children, especially out here on the West Coast, where information relating to the Puerto Rican diaspora and experience, is quite minimal. A useful section, albeit relatively brief, is called Chronology of Important Events, beginning from September 25, 1494, wherein the book states that: "Christopher Columbus leaves the port of Cadiz, Spain, with seventeen ships and between 1,200 and 1,500 sailors".

Also interesting is the books discussion of the derivation of the term "Boriqueña", as the word was first used by the Taíno Indians (See: Los Taínos) on the island, as well as the several different spelling of this word. Thus, this useful book reminds us, that we as a people, had a rich history considerably before the Spaniards ever arrived on this "Enchanted Island". This book can be read and/or obtained in most, if not all, public libraries, and major book stores.

By: Daniel M. López (Feb. 2000)
