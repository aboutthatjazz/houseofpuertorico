Cultural Corner - El Boricua Jan/Feb 2000

Town of the Month: ...... Jayuya

Se fundó como poblado en el 1883 y en 1911 se constituyó como municipio. Su nombre se deriva del cacique Hayuya. Sus barrios son: Jauca, Pica, Zamas, Collores, Saliente, Veguitas, Coabey, Río Grande, Mameyes Arriba, y Jayuya Abajo. Entre sus personalidades se encuentran: Nemesio R. Canales y Carlos Orama Padilla. En noviembre se celebra el Festival Nacional Indígena.

Recipe of the Month:

Plátanos Maduros (Amarillos) al Horno

1. Escoja plátanos bien maduros.

2. Móndelos y déle tres cortes diagonales y poco profundo por ambos lados.

3. Póngalos en un recipiente con agua que los cubra y a la cual le habrá agregado 1 cucharada de sal por cada litro de agua.

4. Déjelos en esta agua alrededor de 1 hora. Sáquelos y escúrralos.

5. Fróteles mantequilla u oleomargarina a todo alrededor.

6. Engrase un molde llano de cristal para hornear y coloque los plátanos en él.

7. Empolvoréelos con azúcar y encima póngales pequeños pedacitos de matequilla.

8. Métalos al horno a una temperatura moderada de 350 grados durante media hora.

9. Sáquelos, vírelos del otro lado y empolvoréelos de nuevo con azúcar y póngales encima otros trocitos de mantequilla.

10. Métalos al horno por otros 30 minutos.

11. Sáquelos del molde y sírvalos en una fuente.

!Buen Provecho!

Article of the Month: ......... Latino Periodicals

There exists one source that assists Boricua parents to help in their children's school work, as well as increase their child's educational and cultural awareness of being Hispanic/Latino, and that book is entitled: Latino Periodicals: A Selection Guide, by Salvador Guerena and Vivian M. Pisano, eds. (1998). This comparative book lists 300 Spanish - and English - language publications, from professional journals and newsletters to fashion magazines and fotonovelas. Information on price, circulation, frequency published, and subscription services is provided, along with a description and review of a recent issue of the periodical. The main arrangement is by broad subject or type and thorough indexing provides access by title and topic.

This book, although specifically geared to Librarians, it is also a very useful tool for parents, as well as for their children, relatives and friends. Thus, this book appeals to Spanish-speaking, bilingual, and English-speaking persons. Of particular note is that outside of the many Spanish-language comic books geared to children, currently there do not appear to be, with the exception of Chispa, any magazines oriented to Latino children.

Examples of the broad topics/chapters that are covered, all "Latino-oriented," are: Bibliographies and indexes; General interest; Latin America; Business and professional; Current events; Literature and culture; Politics and social issues; Parenting; Sports and physical fitness; Focus on women; Focus on teens; Fotonovelas; Newspapers (U.S. domestic); Latin American newspapers; and Newsletters.

Although their is a paucity of specific entries devoted to Boricua issues, one should be able to find sufficient articles relative to the Puerto Rican experience within any number of these publications cited within this comprehensive, practical and useful "Latino-oriented" book.

Finally, a short listing of periodicals, magazines and Newsletters that are listed are: El Heraldo de Chicago; Hispania; Hispanic Business Magazine; Hispanic Engineer; The Hispanic outlook in Higher Education, Hispanic times Magazine, El Hispano News; Latina; Latina Magazine Bilingüe; Latina Style; Leading Hispanics; National Directory of Hispanic Organizations; El Nueva Dia; La Opinion; Journal of Latin American Studies; California Association for Bilingual Education Newsletter and La Prensa San Diego.
